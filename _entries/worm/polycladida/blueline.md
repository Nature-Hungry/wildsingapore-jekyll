## Where seen?
This small pale flatworm elegantly edged in blue with a central blue stripe is regularly encountered on many of our shores, on coral rubble near living reefs. 'Concinnus' means 'elegant' in Latin.

## Features

2-4cm long. There is a pair of pseudotentacles made up of simple folded edges of the body.

Grouped in this entry are flatworms with a body that is white, beige or pale bluish, edged with a bright blue margin and one central blue stripe along the length of the body. The central line ends with a tiny blue circle-dot near the head.

From Rene Ong:

*Pseudoceros concinnus*
: central line is all solid blue

*Yellow striped flatworm Pseudoceros*
sp. 3: central line is yellow with dark blue border

*Braided stripe flatworm Pseudoceros*
sp. 4: resembles Pseudoceros sp. 3 but has a golden-speckled yellow line that looks intermittent by some dull orange and ends in front.

Racing stripe flatworm Pseudoceros bifurcus - Bluish body with central stripe that is white or yellow becoming orange or red near the head, the stripe is edged with a thin dark purple border.
