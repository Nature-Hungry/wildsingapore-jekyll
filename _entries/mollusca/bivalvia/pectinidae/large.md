**Where seen?**
These large clams are sometimes seen on our Southern shores near living reefs.

**Features:**
Diameter 6-10cm. A circular two-part shell, thick and heavy, yellowish, often covered in sediments. When submerged, a fringe of tentacles emerge, some long and many short tentacles, with many tiny, well developed eyes along the mantle edge.

**Clap back clam**
**:**
It can 'swim' backwards with claps of its shell.
