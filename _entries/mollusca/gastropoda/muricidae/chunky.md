**Where seen?**
This heavy thick-shelled drill is often seen on large boulders and seawalls. Commonly seen on our all our shores.

**Features:**
3-6cm. Shell thick and chunky with variable patterns, some with a spiral pattern of squarish bumps, some with blunt spines. Shell opening wide. The operculum is brown.

Various species of
*Thais*
commonly seen on our shores have this kind of shells. They are difficult to distinguish with certainty in the field. On this website, they are grouped by external features for convenience of display.

**Drill Babies:**
Some drills lay clusters of bright yellow egg capsules on hard surfaces. The egg capsules turn purple when the free-swimming larvae hatch.

{% include img-wrap-group.html items=page.galleries.gallery2 %}**Species are difficult to positively identify without close examination.

On this website, they are grouped by external features for convenience of display.*
