## Where seen?
This drill is often seen in groups on large boulders on our Nothern shores. Sometimes, groups of small ones are seen under large stones.

## Features

2-3cm. Shell thick but not as thick as those of
[Chunky drills](chunky)
. With variable patterns, some with spiralling grooves, others with
a spiral pattern of bumps
or spiral of small blunt spines.
Shell opening wide. Operculum orange or yellow.

Various species of
*Thais*
commonly seen on our shores have this kind of shells. They are difficult to distinguish with certainty in the field. On this website, they are grouped by external features for convenience of display.

**Drill Babies:**
Some drills lay clusters of bright yellow egg capsules on hard surfaces. The egg capsules turn purple when the free-swimming larvae hatch.
