## Where seen?
Resembling a large stiff white slug, this snail is sometimes seen in our seagrass meadows and sandy shores at night. When the shell is seen, it looks like a moon snail that refuses to withdraw into its shell. It is also called the Ear moon snail or Baby ear moon snail because of the shape of its delicate shell.

## Features

The animal can be 10cm long. Shell small (about 2cm) thin, white and flat with a wide shell opening and is usually completely covered by the living animal. Body leathery and white, huge and unable to retract completely into the shell. The animal can produce a large amount of slime.
