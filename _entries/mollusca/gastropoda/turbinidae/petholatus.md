## Where seen?
Rarely seen, this pretty snails is found in living reefs on our Southern shores. It is more active at night.
## Features

About 6cm. Shell thick smooth glossy, without any cords. Colours brown, with a spiralling pattern of fine banded lines. Operculum shelly, hemi-spherical and smooth, brown and greenish. Body black with a bright orange foot, a pair of slender tentacles.

**Sometimes confused with**
the
[Top shell snail](../trochidae/trochidae)
(Family Trochidae) has a more pyramidal shell and a thin operculum made of a horn-like material. While the turban shell snail has a shell with more distinct whorls and a thick, chalky operculum. Here's more on
[how to tell apart turban and top shell snails](../topturban)
.

**Human uses:**
It is listed as Endangered on the Red List of threatened animals of Singapore. Although never abundant, it could be found up until the early 1970s.
