## Where seen?
This mushroom coral with is sometimes seen on some of our undisturbed Southern shores. The coral is free-living (it is not attached to the surface) and large ones may be seen on shallow sandy or rubbly areas near living reefs. Usually seen alone.

## Features

Oval to elongated convex disks with rounded ends, to about 15-25cm long. Prominent central furrow. Some species may have only one mouth, while the other species may have several, found both in and outside the central furrow. Upper surface with thin lines radiating from the centre, with fine 'teeth' which are usually not obvious when the lines are covered with fleshy tissue. Lines parallel, mostly continuous. Tentacles are tiny and very sparse. Tentacles may be extended both during the day and at night. The underside usually concave. Colours seen usually beige or pinkish brown or yellowish brown.
