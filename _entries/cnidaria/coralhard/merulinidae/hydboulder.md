**Boulder
horn coral** ***Hydnophora exesa*\*  
Family Merulinidae***updated
Nov 2019*

---

**Where
seen?** This boulder-shaped hard coral with conical bumps
are sometimes seen on our Southern shores.  
  
**Features:** Colonies (15-20cm, sometimes much larger), boulder-shaped with irregular
lumps and hillocks. The unique feature of the *Hydnophora* corals
are the small conical mounds (0.5cm or smaller), called monticules
(also hydnae or hydnophores), that form where the corallite walls
of adjacent polyps fuse together. In these boulder-shaped colonies,
monticules may be conical short, or long, forming valleys along the
surface of the colony. Polyps have short blunt tentacles that surround
the base of each monticule. The skeleton is hidden when the tentacles
are expanded. Colours seen include brown, green and blue.
