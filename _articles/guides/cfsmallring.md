****Hard
corals with small rings****How
to tell them apart?***updated
Nov 11***

---

Some corals
with small ring-shaped corallites may look similar. Here's more on how to tell them apart.

---

![](merulinidae/caulastraea/040522kusbd0064m3.jpg)  
[**Trumpet
coral**](merulinidae/caulastraea.htm)  
Colony appears
boulder-shaped,   
but is not solid.

![](merulinidae/caulastraea/040522kusad0062m3.jpg)  
Corallites large trumpet-shaped, long narrow flaring to oval opening.

---

![](faviidae/diploastrea/070704jngg1249m3.jpg)  
[**Moon coral**](diploastrea.htm)   
Colony encrusting to boulder-shaped.

![](faviidae/oulastrea/050611kusg7803m3.jpg)  
[**Zebra
coral**](faviidae/oulastrea.htm)  
Colony encrusting,  
sometimes small boulder-shaped.

![](merulinidae/cyphastrea/120124sisd5992m3.jpg)  
**[Meteor coral](merulinidae/cyphastrea.htm)**Colony boulder-shaped,  
sometimes in hillocks.

![](faviidae/diploastrea/070704jngg1249m3a.jpg)  
Corallites neat domes with small opening and fine neat radiating
lines.

![](faviidae/oulastrea/050611kusg7803m3b.jpg)  
Corallites circular ring with thick white radiating walls on a black
background.

![](merulinidae/cyphastrea/120124sisd5996m3.jpg)  
Corallites a tiny circular ring often not packed tightly, with tiny
bumps between the corallites.
