## Main Issues

### Ecological Connectivity

The site is a highway that animals use to travel between forests down south like Labrador Nature Reserve and Berlayer Creek, to forests up north like Mount Faber Park and Telok Blangah Hill Park. Developing this site considerably might eradicate this ecological connectivity for sensitive species like the [Pangolin](https://avs.nparks.gov.sg/wildlife/encountering-wildlife/pangolins/), [Thick-billed Green Pigeon](https://singaporebirds.com/species/thick-billed-green-pigeon/), and [Square-tailed Drongo-cuckoo](https://singaporebirds.com/species/square-tailed-drongo-cuckoo/).

### Lack of Buffers

The Proposed Retained Area largely overlaps with the [Native-dominated Secondary Forest](/_posts/habitats/native-dominated-secondary-forest.md), which is good news as this forest type is of highest ecological value in the site. However, retaining only the Native-dominated Secondary Forest without any buffers leaves the forest to fend for itself against Forest Edge Effect. This means that overtime, the Native-dominated Secondary Forest can possibly deteriorate naturally as conditions of the site change, like decreases in humidity and increase in wind.
