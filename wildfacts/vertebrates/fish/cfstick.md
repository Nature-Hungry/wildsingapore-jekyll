**Stick-like
fishes   
How
to tell them apart?***updated
Sep 2020*   

---

Several different
kinds of fishes are long and thin and look like sticks.
Here's more on
how to tell them apart.

---

![](belonidae/belonidae/091128cjd4669m3.jpg)  
**[Needflefish](belonidae/belonidae.htm)**Family Belonidae 
![](belonidae/belonidae/091128cjd4669m3a.jpg)  
Long narrow pointy jaws full of sharp teeth. Lower
and upper jaws the same length.
![](belonidae/belonidae/091128cjd4669m3c.jpg)  
 Tail fin small.
Swims
mostly at the water surface. Quite active.
 
 

---

![](hemiramphidae/plain/031030sntd0034m3.jpg)  
**[Halfbeak](hemiramphidae/hemiramphidae.htm)**Family Hemiramphidae 
![](hemiramphidae/plain/031030sntd0034m3a.jpg)  
Sharp
pointed jaws. Upper
jaw much shorter than lower jaws.
![](hemiramphidae/plain/031030sntd0034m3c.jpg)  
Tail fin small.
Swims
mostly at the water surface. Quite active.
 
 

---

![](sphyraenidae/sphyraenidae/110814smkd8897m3.jpg)  
**[Barracuda](sphyraenidae/sphyraenidae.htm)** (Juvenile)Family Sphyraenidae
![](sphyraenidae/sphyraenidae/110814smkd8897m3a.jpg)  
Blunt
pointed jaws. Upper
jaw about the same length as lower jaws.
![](sphyraenidae/sphyraenidae/110814smkd8897m3b.jpg)  
Tail fin small, forked.
Swims
mostly at the water surface. Quite active.
 
 

---

![](centriscidae/centriscidae/080523cyrd4056m3.jpg)  
[**Razorfish**](centriscidae/centriscidae.htm)  
Family Centriscidae
![](centriscidae/centriscidae/080523cyrd4056m3a.jpg)  
Tube-like snout, long, narrow, pointed.
![](centriscidae/centriscidae/080523cyrd4056m3b.jpg)  
Sharp dorsal spine, in some species bent. Fins tiny, transparent under the spine.
Swims head down, with its tail nearer the surface. But can also swim away horizontally.
 
 

---

![](monacanthidae/anacanthus/080523cyrd3973m3.jpg)  
[**Beaded filefish**](monacanthidae/anacanthus.htm)  
Family Monacanthidae
![](monacanthidae/anacanthus/080523cyrd3973m3a.jpg)  
Tiny uptuned mouth. Barbel under the chin
that can be straightened out.
![](monacanthidae/anacanthus/080523cyrd3971m3a.jpg)  
Tail fin large and long.
Among seagrasses and floating seaweeds.
 
 

---

![](syngnathidae/biaculeatus/080523cyrd4008m3.jpg)  
[**Alligator pipefish**](syngnathidae/biaculeatus.htm)  
*Syngnathoides biaculeatus*
![](syngnathidae/biaculeatus/080523cyrd3932m3a.jpg)  
Tube-like snout. Some may have a pair of tiny tentacles at the tip of the snout.
![](syngnathidae/biaculeatus/080523cyrd3895m3c.jpg)  
Tail prehensile, no tail fin.
Usually among seagrasses, not really at the water surface.
 
 

---

![](syngnathidae/seagrass/071001chgd6784m3.jpg)  
**[Seagrass pipefish](syngnathidae/seagrass.htm)**  
*awaiting identification*
![](syngnathidae/seagrass/140812chg4d8517m3.jpg)  
Tube-like snout.
![](syngnathidae/seagrass/071001chgd6784m3c.jpg)  
Tiny tail fin.
Usually among seagrasses, not really at the water surface.
