[Curled-tip  
red seaweed](rhodophyta/portieria) *Portieria hornemannii*

3-6cm.
Cluster of stiff, slender, cylindrical 'stems' regularly branched,
tipped with a white rounded 'cap'. Pink, maroon, purple. Bushy on
rubble and ropes, sometimes a crunchy carpet among seagrasses. Commonly
seen on all our shores.
3-6cm.
Cluster of stiff, thick, cylindrical 'stems' regularly branched, a
darker pink dot at the tip. Pink, maroon. Bushy among living reefs
and on rubble. Commonly seen on our Southern shores.
5-8cm.
Cluster of stiff, thick, cylindrical 'stems' forming Y-shapes, tipped
with a white squarish 'cap'. Pink. Flat layers (not bushy) among living
reefs. Commonly seen on our Southern shores.
 
Short
thin 'stems' finely branched with pointed projections giving an overall
prickly appearance. On coral rubble. Sometimes seen on our Southern
shores.
