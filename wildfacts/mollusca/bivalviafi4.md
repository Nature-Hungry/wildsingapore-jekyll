About
3cm, Shell thin elongated, white, golden to purplish. Found stuck
to mangrove leaves, tree trunks, roots and other hard surfaces.
Commonly seen in our mangroves.
2-
3cm, one side stuck to a rock, pearly, thin shell. Commonly seen on
some of our shores.

3-4cm.
One side of shell stuck to hard surface. Shell thick with long hollow
spikes. Attached to hard surfaces. Common on all our shores.
8-15cm.
Plain shell, shell opening close to the surface, not regularly zig-zag.
Common on rocks on the Northern shores.
3-8cm.
Shell opening away from the surface, regularly zig-zag. Common rocks
on the Southern shores.
 
8-10cm.
Lower valve is cup-shaped and stuck to a hard surface. Upper valve
flat covered with flat short spines. Among living reefs. Sometimes
seen on some of our shores.
