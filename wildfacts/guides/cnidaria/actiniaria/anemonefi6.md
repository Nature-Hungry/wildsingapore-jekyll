*Phymanthus* sp.

4-8cm.
Six of the tentacles near the centre usually of a different colour.
Oral disk pale. Coral rubble. Commonly seen on our Southern shores.
8-12cm.
Bright yellow or white branches on tentacles give it a banded look.
Oral disk unpatterned with spots. Coral rubble. Commonly seen on our Southern shores.
8-12cm.
Tentacles uniformly coloured sometimes the 'branches' are outlined
in white. Oral disk with white bars forming a petal-shaped pattern.
Coral rubble. Commonly seen on our Southern shores.
8-12cm.
Tentacles smooth, but some with small branches visible, most pale
with six coloured ones. Oral disk plain. Coral rubble. Commonly seen
on our Southern shores.

8-12cm.
Tentacles and branches brown, with violet tips. Oral disk with fine
white radiating lines. Coral rubble. Commonly seen on our Southern shores.

[Fire anemone](actiniaria/actinodendron.htm)   
*Actinodendron arboreum*

 
 
 
25-30cm.
Oral disk with raised edge and tiny, tightly packed tentacles on the
surface, underside often colourful. Hidden in crevices or under dead
corals. Seen on our Southern shores.
15-20cm.
Fat fleshy tentacles covered with branches. **Stings
painfully.** On sand near coral rubble. Seen on our Southern shores.
