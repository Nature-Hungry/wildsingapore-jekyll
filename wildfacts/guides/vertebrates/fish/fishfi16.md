![](gobiidae/histrio/051204sisg3318m.jpg)  
[Acropora goby](gobiidae/histrio.htm)  
*Gobiodon histrio*

2-9cm.
Red to reddish-brown with three broad white bars. Lives in large [Giant
carpet anemones](../../cnidaria/actiniaria/gigantea.htm) and [Magnificent
anemones.](../../cnidaria/actiniaria/magnifica.htm) Commonly seen on many of our Southern shores.
5-14cm.
Red to black with one black-edged white bar just behind the head.
Lives in [Bulb-tip
sea anemones](../../cnidaria/actiniaria/entacmaea.htm). Sometimes seen on our Southern shores.
2-11cm.
Juveniles jet black with spot of forehead and sides, adults greyish,
spots faded. Lives in [Giant carpet anemones](../../cnidaria/actiniaria/gigantea.htm). Sometimes seen on some of our Southern shores.
 
4cm.
Colourful stripes. Lives among the branches of a living [acropora
hard coral](../../cnidaria/coralhard/acroporidae/acropora.htm). Sometimes seen on our Southern shores.

![](gobiidae/maudae/050513labg6528m.jpg)  
[Saddled shrimp-goby](gobiidae/maudae.htm)  
*Cryptocentrus maudae*

![](gobiidae/maudae/090625cyrd5636m.jpg)  
[Saddled shrimp-goby](gobiidae/maudae.htm)  
*Cryptocentrus maudae*

![](gobiidae/leptocephalus/080830kusd9939m.jpg)  
[Pink-speckled shrimp-goby](gobiidae/leptocephalus.htm)   
*Cryptocentrus   
leptocephalus*

 
 
About 1.5cm.
Tiny ones with black with 5 or more white saddles. Lives with a [snapping
shrimp](../../crustacea/othercrust/shrimp/alpheidae.htm). Sometimes seen on some of our shores.
5-7cm.
Adults with 5 or more pale saddles, dark spots along the sides and
speckles all over. Lives with a [snapping
shrimp](../../crustacea/othercrust/shrimp/alpheidae.htm). Sometimes seen on our Southern shores.
4-6cm.
6 broad oblique bars on the body, many pink and blue spots on the
head. Lives with a [snapping
shrimp](../../crustacea/othercrust/shrimp/alpheidae.htm). Sandy, silty areas near reefs. Sometimes seen on our Southern
shores.
 
 
  
in
red are those listed among the threatened animals of Singapore
from Davison, G.W. H. and P. K. L. Ng and Ho Hua Chew, 2008. *The Singapore
Red Data Book: Threatened plants and animals of Singapore*.
