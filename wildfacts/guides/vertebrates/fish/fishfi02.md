![](pomacentridae/tripunctatus/080410cyrd0050m.jpg)  
[Three-spot damselfish](pomacentridae/tripunctatus.htm)  
*Pomacentrus tripunctatus*

3-4cm
long. White and reddish stripes on sides forming chequered pattern.
In pools near seagrasses and coral rubble. Sometimes seen on some
of our shores.
5-7cm.
6 red or brown lines on a pale body with a large black spot at the
base of the tail fin. Coral rubble and reefs. Sometimes seen on our
Southern shores.
2-3cm. Juvenile with black-and-white stripes on body and tail. Sometimes
seen near reefs.
2-3cm. Juvenile with three curved blackish stripes on the sides and a striped
tail fin. Near reefs. Sometimes seen on some of our shores.
2-3cm. Juvenile with electric blue stripes on nose and ringed dark
spot on dorsal fin and base of tail. Living reefs. Sometimes seen
on our Northern shores.

3-5cm.
'Chubby face', small eyes. Sometimes seen on some of
our shores.
4-6cm.
Large eyes, three dark brown blotches on the side of the body. Mangroves,
seagrass, coral rubble, also near reefs. Commonly seen on many of
our shores.
4-6cm.
Large eyes, three dark brown blotches on the side of the body. Mangroves,

4-5cm.
Pale body with pairs of close-set round black spots along the body.
Reefs and rubble. Seen on many of our shores.
3-4cm,
to 6cm. Bulbous snout, slender body densely mottled, black bar
under first dorsal fin, black blotch at rear of first dorsal fin.
Commonly seen in our mangroves.

*Dischistodus prosopotaenia*

4-6cm,
to 14cm. With 6-7 black narrow bars across a yellowish body. Tail
no horizontal bars and has rounded lobes. Coral rubble near reefs.
Sometimes seen on our Southern shores.
4-6cm,
to 11cm. With 4 thick yellow bars and 5 thick black bars across the
body. Juveniles look very different from adults. Near living reefs. Sometimes seen on our Southern shores.
 
 
2cm,
to 20cm. Juveniles with a white body, two broad honey brown bars and
a large gold-margined black spot on the dorsal fin. Living reefs. Sometimes
seen on our Southern shores.
