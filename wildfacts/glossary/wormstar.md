**Bristleworm
or star?  
how to tell them apart***updated
Apr 2020*

---

**[Bristleworms](../worm/polychaeta/polychaeta)** are worms that are very bristley.  
  
[**Brittle
stars**](../echinodermata/ophiuroidea/ophiuroidea) have bristley arms, usually five arms sometimes more
or less. The arms are connected to a central disk that is usually
well hidden. With only the arms sticking out, brittle star arms can
be confused for bristleworms.   
  
**[Feather stars](../echinodermata/crinoidea/crinoidea)** also have bristely arms. While brittle stars generally have five arms,
feather stars can have 10 or more arms. Feather stars are not as commonly
seen as brittle stars.
