**Sausage-like
animals****How
to tell them apart?**  
*updated
Apr 2020*

---

 Many sausage-like
animals are seen on our shores, especially at low tide when they are
out of water. They can look very different when they are submerged.
Here's more on how to tell them apart.

---

**[Sea
cucumbers](../echinodermata/holothuroidea/holothuroidea)**Phylum
Echinodermata

**[Sea
pen](../cnidaria/others/pennatulacea/veretillidae)**Phylum
Cnidaria

**[Peacock
anemone](../cnidaria/others/ceriantharia/ceriantharia)**Phylum
Cnidaria

Sea
cucumbers are often sausage shaped. Some have bumps and other texture
on the body. Others may be smooth.
The
flowery sea pen looks   
like a sausage when out of water   
with all the polyps retracted.
Peacock
anemones have   
a long columnar body   
that slides into a soft tube.

*![](../echinodermata/holothuroidea/colochirus/031226chgd0003m3.jpg)*

*![](../cnidaria/others/pennatulacea/veretillidae/020725chgd3953m3.jpg)*

*![](../cnidaria/others/ceriantharia/common/020711chd1962m3.jpg)*

When
submerged, feathery feeding tentacles may appear at the mouth.
When
submerged, the flower-like polyps emerge all along   
the length of the colony.
When
submerged, the long   
tentacles are extended   
into the water.
