**Pulau
Semakau**   

---

**Western
shore habitats** (2.5km x 400m)  
Sandy shores, seagrass meadows, rubble with fringe of sparse corals.  
  
**Eastern shore habitats** (1.5km
x 100m)  
Sandy shores, mudflats, rubble with fringe of sparse corals.  
  
**In the middle** (about 15ha), original mangroves with
mudlobster mounds and [a
possible hot spring](http://wildshores.blogspot.sg/2011/10/semakau-at-high-tide-with-hot-spring.html), coastal forest, secondary forest.

**Conservation
status:** Listed for use as 'Open Space' in the [URA
Master Plan 2008](http://www.ura.gov.sg/uramaps/?config=config_preopen.xml&preopen=Master%20Plan), i.e., "Area to be used or intended to
be used as open space". Status not indicated in [Parks
and Waterbodies Plan](http://www.ura.gov.sg/uramaps/?preopen=Special%20and%20Detailed%20Controls%20Plans%20%28SDCP%29). Access to Pulau Semakau is managed by
the [National Environment
Agency](http://app2.nea.gov.sg/index.aspx) that runs the [Semakau
Landfill](http://app2.nea.gov.sg/semakaulandfill.aspx).Mentioned in the [Singapore
Green Plan 2012](http://app.mewr.gov.sg/data/ImgCont/1342/sgp2012.pdf) under "Marine Nature Areas":

**Current conservation activities:**

- On 16 Jul
  05, Pulau Semakau was opened for nature-related recreational activities.
  Members of the public can now visit the island for birdwatching,
  sports fishing and intertidal walks.
- Since 1998,
  Pulau Semakau has been one of the survey sites of [underwater
  coral reef surveys](http://coralreef.nus.edu.sg/survey/data.htm) by [ReefFriends](http://www.bluewatervolunteers.org/reeffriends/)
  of [NParks](http://www.nparks.gov.sg/cms/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=120&Itemid=123)
  and the [Blue Water
  Volunteers](http://www.bluewatervolunteers.org/).
- Since 2006,
  [TeamSeagrass](http://teamseagrass.blogspot.sg/) has
  been monitoring [seagrasses
  at Pulau Semakau](http://www.seagrasswatch.org/Singapore.html).
- From 2009
  to 2011 [Project
  Semakau](http://rmbr.nus.edu.sg/projectsemakau/) collected data on the biodiversity of Pulau Semakau.
- In 2011,
  the mudflats on Pulau Semakau was surveyed during the [Mega
  Marine Survey](http://megamarinesurvey.blogspot.sg/search/label/pulau%20semakau).
- [A
  book featuring the biodiversity of Pulau Semakau](http://wildshores.blogspot.sg/search/label/project-semakau-book) has been
  published by the National Environment Agency that manages the
  Landfill.

**History:**In 1999, when the last remaining landfill on Singapore�s mainland
was exhausted, the Semakau Landfill was created by enclosing part
of Pulau Semakau and all of a small adjacent island (Pulau Sakeng)
with a rock bund. All of Singapore's waste now ends up in this Landfill,
i.e., ash from the incineration plants and all non-incinerable waste.
The parts of Pulau Semakau that was not destroyed remains natural.

**About
the name:** *Pulau*=Island; *Semakau*=unknown,
but *semak*=scrub or undergrowth.
